## 2. Strongest objections

**A. The childcare line is inconsistent with the "$0 spouse" premise — and it hides the real year‑two cost.**
Year one assumes the spouse "earns **$0 the first year**" and is home job‑hunting — yet childcare is budgeted at **$1,350/mo**, *higher* than today's $1,150. The budget holds childcare flat regardless of the spouse's employment state, which is the one variable that drives it. Net effect: year one looks slightly too grim, year two looks much too rosy — and the bias points toward "go."

**B. The year‑two "flips strongly positive" is overstated on two independent counts.**
The claim: a **$42,000** job makes "household take‑home rise roughly **+$2,590/month**." That's $31,080/yr net on $42k gross — **74% retention** on income *stacked on top of $176k in a high‑tax state*. The marginal rate on that second income is ~35–40% (fed + high state + FICA), so realistic net is **~$2,100–2,300/mo, not $2,590** — an overstatement of ~$300–450/mo. Then subtract the new working‑parent childcare from objection A. The honest year‑two improvement is roughly **half** the advertised swing. The picture still turns positive, but "strongly" is doing unearned work.

**C. "Take‑home slightly higher than now" is true only at the optimistic edge of the tax assumption.**
You're claiming $176k single‑earner in a high‑tax coastal state nets **$10,267/mo** (30.0% effective deduction) vs today's $9,990 (26.0%). That's plausible but optimistic: push the effective rate to ~32% (very reachable in CA/NY‑class states on a concentrated single income) and take‑home falls to **~$9,970 — flat to slightly *down***, which erases the "raise more than offsets" framing entirely. The headline rests on a 1–2 point tax estimate.

**D. The raise is nominal, not real. Frame this as buying an option, not getting a raise.**

**E. The sign‑on bonus is pre‑tax; it does not actually cover the move.**
"A flat **$20,000 sign‑on**… the bonus roughly covers the move" against a **$18,000** estimate. Sign‑on bonuses are taxable (supplemental ~22% fed + FICA + state withholding), so $20k gross nets ~$13–14k. There's a **~$4–5k hole** that comes straight out of the $70k savings — before any moving‑cost overrun, and moves routinely overrun.
