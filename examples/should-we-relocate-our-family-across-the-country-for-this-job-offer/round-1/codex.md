**1. Verdict**

Caution, medium-high confidence. This is feasible only if they turn the move from a prestige/career bet into a managed transition with negotiated cash protection, verified housing/childcare costs, and a spouse re-employment plan with dates. A signed spouse job, stronger relocation package, or verified lower housing would move this toward `ship`; weak employer flexibility or higher actual costs would move it toward `block`.

**2. Strongest Objections**

- The year-one margin is too thin: `$537/month` savings leaves almost no room for rent variance, childcare gaps, parking, insurance increases, travel back to family, medical surprises, or a delayed spouse job.
- The sign-on likely does not “roughly cover” the move after tax. IRS 2026 guidance says ordinary moving reimbursements are taxable, and taxable benefits must be included in pay unless excluded by law. Source: [IRS Publication 15-B](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p15b).
- The family is giving up hidden economic value: nearby family childcare, local support, and spouse employment continuity. The budget prices dollars, but not fragility.
- The upside depends heavily on spouse re-employment, but the plan has no evidence on local job availability, licensing, schedule fit, childcare coverage, or expected time-to-offer.
- Career upside is asserted, not de-risked. “Big-name employer” and “title bump” matter only if the role is stable, promotion path is real, manager quality is good, and a failed move still improves future employability.

**3. Recommended Execution Sequence**

1. Rebuild the model using net cash: actual payroll estimate, state/local taxes, benefits, retirement contributions, sign-on withholding, relocation clawback, deposits, and first-year one-time setup costs.
2. Negotiate before acceptance: grossed-up relocation/sign-on, temporary housing, lease-break coverage, childcare support, delayed start, hybrid/remote ramp, severance or guaranteed bonus treatment if laid off.
3. Verify the new city budget with real quotes: 5-10 rental comps, school/aftercare availability, summer care, commute costs, car insurance, parking, healthcare premiums, and travel-to-family costs.
4. Run spouse job discovery before moving: applications, recruiter calls, local pay bands, required credentials, childcare-compatible schedules, and a 90/180/270-day job-search plan.
5. Decide with gates: accept only if minimum cash runway, rent cap, childcare slot, and employer terms are met.
6. Move in phases if possible: primary earner starts first, family follows after housing/school/childcare are locked.

**4. Invariants And Guardrails**

- Do not move unless post-move liquid savings stays above a defined floor, preferably at least 9-12 months of new-city essential expenses.
- Do not count the `$20,000` sign-on at gross value; use after-tax cash and account for clawback.
- Do not sign a lease above the modeled rent without re-approving the whole plan.
- Do not assume spouse income until there is either an offer, credible pipeline, or a cash runway that survives 12-18 months without it.
- Preserve exit options: avoid buying a house in year one, avoid new debt, and keep enough cash to move back or pivot.
