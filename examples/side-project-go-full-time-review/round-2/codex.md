The plan is too product-build heavy. A Team plan, integrations marketplace, and paid acquisition are three major execution tracks for a solo founder. The next constraint is probably distribution and economics, not engineering output.

The `$15/mo` plan leaves little room for CAC. With 5% monthly churn, simple lifetime is about 20 months, or roughly `$300` gross LTV before costs. That means paid channels need very low CAC or materially higher ARPA.

The current hard stop is too late and too blunt. “Not at `$8k MRR` by month 12” ignores runway floor, channel proof, churn, ARPA, and the fact that getting another senior SWE job may take months.

**4. Recommended Execution Sequence**

Next 12 weeks, while employed:

1. Build the true cash model: COBRA/ACA, taxes, business costs, retirement/401k loss, support tools, and a non-spendable savings floor.
2. Instrument the business: gross churn, net churn, cohorts, activation, invoice volume, support load, gross new MRR, churned MRR, and source attribution.
3. Run pricing/ARPA tests before major features: annual plan, `$19-29` new-customer pricing, Pro tier, paid add-ons, or Team pre-sales at `$49-99`.
4. Interview and pre-sell to the users most likely to pay more. Do not build the marketplace until customers commit to specific integrations.
5. Run small acquisition tests with hard caps. Paid acquisition only continues if CAC payback is under about 6 months on gross margin.
6. At vesting, decide based on gates, not vibes. If gates miss, keep the job and use the vesting event to buy time or negotiate part-time/contract flexibility.

Suggested quit gates: at least `$5.2-5.8k MRR`, churn trending below 4% or clearly improving by cohort, proof of ARPA expansion above `$15`, one repeatable acquisition motion, and written partner agreement on runway/floor.
