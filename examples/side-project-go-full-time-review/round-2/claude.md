## 3. Strongest remaining objections

**(A) Even at the $12k target, he replaces ~half his total comp — not his salary.**
$12k MRR = **$144k/yr gross revenue**, which is not income. Strip the stack:
- Payment fees (~3%): –$4.3k · Hosting/tooling: –$4k · Self-employment tax delta (employer FICA ~7.65% on net SE income): –~$10k · **Self-paid family health insurance: –$18–26k/yr** (was employer-subsidized) · Lost 401k match: –$5–8k · Forgone forward equity vesting: –$22k/yr.

Net personal economic value at the **target** ≈ **$100–110k**, versus current **$165k salary + ~$22k equity + benefits ≈ $200k+ total comp**. **He is trading down to ~50% of total comp even if the plan fully succeeds.** The income case never closes; the only case that closes is the **asset** case (see B).

**(B) The real upside is an asset, and the plan's logic for building it is broken.**
A SaaS at $10–12k MRR with 5% churn, organic-only distribution, and solo key-person risk realistically sells for **~2–3× annual profit → roughly $150–350k**, *if* sellable. That asset — not income — is the prize. But the plan funds it by adding **coding capacity** (Team plan, integrations marketplace), when the growth engine is **organic distribution**. **Spending the scarcest resource (25 founder-hours/week) on the non-bottleneck is the central economic error.**

**(C) The target requires acceleration the evidence doesn't support; the hard-stop is nearly free.**
At the *observed* 8% net rate: $4,200 × 1.08⁹ = **$8,395** at month 9 (target is $12k) and × 1.08¹² = **$10,577** at month 12. To hit **$12k by month 9 he needs ~12.3% MoM net** (+54% faster) **while the base is 2–3× larger** and the non-repeating Reddit/IH spikes are spent. Meanwhile the **$8k hard-stop at month 12 needs only ~5.5% MoM** — it clears even if growth *decelerates*. So the floor is safe and the target is fantasy; the plan is anchored to the wrong number.

## 4. Recommended execution sequence (months 1–3, still employed)

1. **Re-cost true burn.** Get an actual marketplace/COBRA family quote and add SE-tax delta. Expect true burn ≈ **$8,000–8,500/mo**, not $6,500.
2. **Pre-sell ARPA, don't build it.** Do *not* write the Team plan yet. Take the existing 280 customers and get **paid commitments / LOIs** for a higher tier. ARPA lift validated by money, not by code. This is the highest-leverage test of the whole thesis.
3. **Run a $1–2k paid-acquisition probe** on 1–2 channels to measure **real CAC**. The entire paid-growth leg is gated on this.
4. **Decompose the 5% churn:** voluntary vs involuntary (failed cards), and cohort shape. Involuntary churn is cheap to recover via dunning and may be a free point of net growth.
5. **Decouple the trigger from the vest.** Bank the $22k regardless; make *quitting* conditional on gates 2–3 passing — not on the calendar.

## 5. Invariants & guardrails

- **Hard re-entry floor:** keep ~**$30k** (≈ 6+ months of *net* household draw at true burn, partner income netted) **untouchable** — it funds a 3–4 month job search, not the business. (Higher than gemini's figure because true burn is higher.)
- **Business firewall:** savings fund **household runway only**. Paid ads are funded from Tally's own revenue, never from the $92k.
- **Paid spend gated on proof:** no scaling until a test shows **CAC < ~$90 and payback < 6 months**.
- **ARPA gate:** do not quit until a tier above $15 is proven liftable via real pre-sales.
- **Dual re-entry trigger (AND→OR):** re-enter if savings hit the floor **OR** month-12 MRR < $8k — whichever comes first. Don't wait for both.
- **Partner alignment:** the floor and re-entry trigger are an explicit, written household agreement, not an implicit one.
