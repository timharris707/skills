# Should I quit my job to go full-time on my side project?

I need a clear-eyed second opinion before I make this call — not a pep talk, and not doom.

## The situation
- I'm a senior software engineer, 34, in a stable job paying **$165k/year**. I don't hate it, but the spark is gone.
- For the last **14 months** I've been building **Tally**, a SaaS tool that helps freelancers automate invoicing and chase late payments.
- Tally is live and earning: **$4,200 MRR** across ~280 paying customers (a $15/mo plan), growing **~8% month-over-month** for the last 5 months.
- Monthly churn is ~5%. Growth is almost entirely organic (SEO + a few well-received Indie Hackers / Reddit posts). I put in ~15 hrs/week, nights and weekends.

## The money
- Cash savings: **$70k**. No debt except a mortgage (**$2,100/mo**).
- Household burn: ~**$6,500/mo** (mortgage, groceries, one kid in daycare at $1,600/mo).
- My partner works and brings in **$4,000/mo** after tax.

## The plan
1. Give notice in **3 months**, right after my next vesting date (~$22k).
2. Go full-time on Tally. Target **$12k MRR within 9 months** by shipping a Team plan, an integrations marketplace, and starting paid acquisition.
3. Hard stop: if I'm not at **$8k MRR by month 12**, I go back to a salaried job.

## What I'm worried about
- Am I romanticizing this? The MRR is real but small, and $15/mo is a thin plan.
- Does 8% MoM growth on a $15 product ever realistically reach salary replacement, or am I extrapolating a hobby?
- Daycare + mortgage make the runway math tighter than the $70k headline suggests.
- I have one shot at the "leave on good terms with vesting" timing.

Tell me straight: is this a smart, derisked bet — or am I about to make an expensive mistake? If you'd change the plan before I pull the trigger, what specifically would you change, and what would you want to see in the next 3 months to feel confident either way?
