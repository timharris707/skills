1. **Verdict**: Caution, medium-high confidence. This is a real business, not a fantasy, but the plan is not derisked enough to quit exactly as written. I’d change to “quit only if 3-month gates pass,” not “quit after vesting.” What would change my view: proof that ARPA can rise above the $15 plan and that acquisition is repeatable beyond organic posts.

2. **Strongest Objections**
- $12k MRR is not salary replacement. It is $144k annual gross revenue before taxes, benefits, business expenses, support, failed ad spend, and retirement savings, versus a $165k salary.
- The growth target requires acceleration. $4.2k MRR growing 8% MoM reaches about $8.4k in 9 months and $10.6k in 12 months. Hitting $12k in 9 months needs about 12.4% net MoM growth.
- 5% monthly churn is the biggest warning light. That implies roughly 54% annual retention if it stays constant. Paid acquisition on a $15/mo product is unforgiving.
- The proposed roadmap is too broad for the risk window: Team plan, integrations marketplace, and paid acquisition are three different bets. The marketplace especially sounds like a maintenance and support trap unless demand is already proven.
- The hard stop is weak. “$8k MRR by month 12” may still be well below a sustainable owner-income threshold, and waiting until month 12 to restart salaried work assumes reemployment is immediate.

3. **Recommended Execution Sequence**
- Next 3 months: stay employed, collect the vest, and run a validation sprint.
- Replace the marketplace idea with pricing and packaging tests: $29-$49 solo/pro tiers, annual prepay, and a narrow Team MVP only if customers ask for it.
- Interview 30-50 current customers, especially churned users, high-usage users, and anyone asking for collaboration or integrations.
- Run small paid acquisition tests with a hard cap. Do not scale unless CAC payback is under 6 months on retained customers.
- Track net MRR, gross new MRR, churn by cohort, ARPA, activation, support load, and owner-distributable cash.
- At the vesting date, choose among: quit, negotiate part-time/contract, or keep running Tally nights/weekends. Do not let the vesting date become the decision criterion.

4. **Invariants And Guardrails**
- Do not quit unless Tally can cover household gap after tax reserves and business expenses, not just on top-line MRR.
- Keep personal runway separate from business cash and ad budget.
- Reserve for taxes. The IRS says self-employed people generally pay income tax, self-employment tax, and quarterly estimated taxes. Source: IRS self-employed tax center, updated May 29, 2026. ([irs.gov](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/self-employed-individuals-tax-center))
- No scaled paid acquisition until CAC payback is proven.
- Start job-search preparation by month 9 if the trajectory is not clearly working; do not wait for the month-12 hard stop.
- If churn stays near 5%, the plan must become ARPA/retention-first before growth-first.

5. **Risks, Stale Assumptions, Missing Evidence**
- Missing: gross margin, hosting/tooling costs, taxes, health insurance, partner income stability, and business cash needs.
- Missing: whether 8% MoM is net of churn and whether it is cohort-stable or just a short organic spike.
- Missing: proof that freelancers want a Team plan.
- Missing: proof that paid acquisition works at $15/mo.
- Stale assumption: returning to a senior SWE role may be possible but not instant. BLS projects strong long-term software employment growth, but that does not prove your local, senior, timing-specific fallback. ([bls.gov](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/software-developers.htm))
